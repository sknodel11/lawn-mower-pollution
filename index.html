Lawn Mower Emissions Reduction Tool – Fort Collins, CO

1. Embodied Noticing

  • Direct observations from the artifact include close contact with a source of pollution, the pollutants are released into the outdoor atmosphere, and the pollutants are released for a finite amount of time.
  • The two inferences that can be made are that the person operating the lawn mower will inhale a certain amount of pollutants, and that the pollutants are negative for human health and the environment
  • An unknown is how much pollutants are released from the lawn mower
  • The question: How much pollutants are released when a lawn mower is being operated, and how can the emissions inhaled and/or released be reduced?
  • The two alternative solutions attempting to address the engineering question above are to either switch to electric lawn mowers or to mow lawns only during times when air mixing is highest

Alternative Comparison

Electric Lawn Mower

Technical Effectiveness: Near elimination of PM2.5, VOCs, and CO emissions.
Environmental: Reduced local pollution but introduces upstream electricity and battery impacts.
Public Health: Significant reduction in direct exposure for operator and nearby residents.
Cost & Practicality: $300–$800 upfront, battery replacement required.

Scheduled Hours (12–4 PM)

Technical Effectiveness: No reduction in emissions, but concentrations drop due to higher mixing height (~1200 m) and wind (~3.5 m/s).
Environmental: No added environmental burden.
Public Health: Reduced exposure due to better dispersion.
Cost & Practicality: Minimal cost, requires behavior change.

Why These Pollutants Are Harmful

  • PM2.5: Penetrates deep into lungs causing respiratory and cardiovascular disease
  • VOCs: Contribute to ozone formation and can be toxic/carcinogenic
  • CO: Reduces oxygen delivery in bloodstream

Modeling Framework & Data

Single-Zone Box Model: C = E / (V × k)

E = emission rate × time
V = area × mixing height
k = wind speed / characteristic length
